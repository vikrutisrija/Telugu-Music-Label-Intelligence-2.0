Most Telugu labels have already won audience scale. The next growth phase is not acquiring more listeners — it is converting existing listeners into communities, memberships, creator ecosystems, and recurring revenue streams.

Fan Ecosystem

Every subscriber is somewhere on this journey. The biggest revenue is created in the later stages—not by getting more listeners, but by converting existing listeners into loyal communities and paying fans.

Every subscriber is somewhere on this journey. The biggest revenue is in the later stages — and getting there requires intentional strategy, not luck. Click any stage to see what most labels do now, what they should do instead, and the immediate action to take.

1

Discovery

~100% of subscribers

+

WHAT MOST LABELS DO NOW

Songs are uploaded to YouTube and streaming platforms. The label waits for the algorithm to recommend them. Growth is mostly passive.

WHAT THEY SHOULD DO

Use SEO-friendly titles, Telugu keywords, teaser campaigns and creator seeding before every release.

REVENUE AT THIS STAGE

No direct revenue yet. This stage determines how many people enter your ecosystem.

IMMEDIATE ACTION

Optimize every video title for Telugu search. Use artist name, movie name and song title separately.

2

Interest

~40% of subscribers

+

WHAT MOST LABELS DO NOW

Occasional playlist additions. Most labels rely on listeners returning on their own without building an ongoing relationship.

WHAT THEY SHOULD DO

Capture fan emails, share artist stories between releases, and keep audiences engaged even when no new music is launching.

REVENUE AT THIS STAGE

Revenue is still limited, but this stage determines how many listeners can become long-term fans.

IMMEDIATE ACTION

Add a fan sign-up link in every YouTube description. Even a small email list creates a direct audience outside social platforms.

3

Engagement

~12% of subscribers

+

WHAT MOST LABELS DO NOW

Fans comment on videos, but labels rarely reply or encourage discussion.

WHAT THEY SHOULD DO

Reply to comments, pin fan content, run polls and encourage fan-created videos.

REVENUE AT THIS STAGE

Higher engagement directly increases sponsorship value and organic reach.

IMMEDIATE ACTION

Reply to every comment for the next 60 days and encourage conversations.

4

Community

~3% of subscribers

+

WHAT MOST LABELS DO NOW

Fan groups exist but are usually run by fans, not the label.

WHAT THEY SHOULD DO

Create official memberships, Discord servers or WhatsApp communities owned by the label.

REVENUE AT THIS STAGE

Memberships generate recurring monthly revenue and stronger loyalty.

IMMEDIATE ACTION

Launch a ₹99/month membership with early song access and exclusive content.

5

Purchase

~0.8% of subscribers

+

WHAT MOST LABELS DO NOW

Merchandise is launched occasionally with no structured commerce strategy.

WHAT THEY SHOULD DO

Launch exclusive merchandise tied to every major release.

REVENUE AT THIS STAGE

Engaged fans are willing to purchase premium experiences and merchandise.

IMMEDIATE ACTION

Create a limited-edition merchandise drop with your next release.

6

Advocacy

~0.15% of subscribers

+

WHAT MOST LABELS DO NOW

Fan pages and edits grow organically without recognition from the label.

WHAT THEY SHOULD DO

Build an official fan ambassador program with recognition and early access.

REVENUE AT THIS STAGE

Advocates become your most effective marketing channel with almost zero acquisition cost.

IMMEDIATE ACTION

Identify your top 100 fan accounts and reward them with exclusive access and recognition.
